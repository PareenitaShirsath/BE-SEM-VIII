## Theory

### Social Media Platforms

- **Facebook:**  
  A social networking website that allows users to connect with friends, family, and other people who share similar interests.

- **Twitter:**  
  A microblogging platform that allows users to send and read short messages called “tweets”.

- **Instagram:**  
  A photo and video sharing app that allows users to share pictures and videos with their followers.

- **TikTok:**  
  A short-form video sharing app that allows users to create and share 15-second videos.

- **LinkedIn:**  
  A professional networking platform that allows users to connect with other professionals in their industry.

- **Pinterest:**  
  A visual discovery platform that allows users to discover and save creative ideas for various projects and interests.

- **Snapchat:**  
  A multimedia messaging app that allows users to send and receive photos and videos that disappear after they’re viewed.

- **YouTube:**  
  A video sharing platform that allows users to upload, share, and view videos.

- **Reddit:**  
  A social news and discussion platform that allows users to submit and vote on content.

- **WhatsApp:**  
  A messaging app that allows users to send and receive text, photos, and videos in real time.

- **Quora:**  
  An online question answering portal.

### Social Media Analytics Tools

- **Google Analytics:**  
  Google Analytics is a cloud-based web analytics service that delivers different tools to optimize digital marketing. It provides real-time, statistical-based analytics solutions about user interaction with a social media website or app.

- **YouTube Analytics:**  
  Allows tracking and monitoring of crucial metrics and statistics for better optimization and performance. It provides insights such as audience retention, real-time activities, and viewer behavior.

- **HubSpot:**  
  HubSpot is one of the most popular social media analytics tools used by companies to manage and attract customers, generate leads, and provide services. It offers tools to create and host web pages, blogs, emails, and manage customer interactions.

- **Pinterest Analytics:**  
  Pinterest Analytics categorizes analytics into Profile, Audience, and Website Analytics.  
  - Profile Analytics shows the most liked pins, visibility, and engagement.  
  - Audience Analytics shows demographics such as gender, location, and interests.  
  These insights help in deciding future content strategies.

- **LinkedIn Analytics:**  
  LinkedIn Analytics helps analyze and measure performance dynamics. It provides analytics about followers, visitors, and connections, helping businesses understand audience engagement.

### Applications of Social Media Analytics for Business

- **Sentiment Analysis**
  - Used to analyze customer sentiment toward a business or product.
  - Provides insights into how people feel about a product, service, or brand.

- **Audience Segmentation**
  - Helps segment audiences based on demographics, interests, and behavior.
  - Enables targeted marketing strategies.

- **Brand Monitoring**
  - Tracks mentions of a business or brand on social media.
  - Helps understand public perception of the company.

- **Campaign Analysis**
  - Measures the success of marketing campaigns.
  - Identifies areas for improvement.

- **Content Analysis**
  - Tracks performance of different content types.
  - Helps identify the most effective content formats.

- **Market Research**
  - Provides insights into market trends, customer needs, and preferences.

- **Social Listening**
  - Monitors social media conversations related to business or industry.
  - Helps identify growth opportunities and stay updated on industry trends.

- **Crisis Management**
  - Helps identify and respond quickly to negative press or customer complaints.

- **Competitor Analysis**
  - Monitors competitors’ activities and strategies.
  - Provides insights into comparative performance.

- **Customer Service**
  - Tracks and responds to customer inquiries and feedback in real time.
  - Improves customer satisfaction and loyalty.

## Aim - Designing and Backtesting Complex Trading Strategies

## Theory

A **trading strategy** is a systematic method for buying and selling financial assets based on predefined rules. Designing a **complex trading strategy** involves combining **technical indicators, fundamental analysis, and risk management** to maximize profitability.

**Backtesting** is the process of evaluating a trading strategy using historical market data before applying it in real-world trading. It helps determine whether the strategy would have been profitable in the past.

---

## Components of a Trading Strategy

### a) Entry and Exit Rules

These rules define when to enter and exit a trade.

**Technical Indicators**
- Moving Averages  
- RSI (Relative Strength Index)  
- MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)  
- Bollinger Bands  

**Chart Patterns**
- Head and Shoulders  
- Double Top and Double Bottom  
- Trendlines  

**Quantitative Models**
- Statistical Arbitrage  
- Mean Reversion Strategy  
- Momentum Strategy  

---

### b) Risk Management

Risk management helps protect trading capital and control potential losses.

**Position Sizing**
- Determines the amount of capital used in each trade.  
- Example: Risk only **2% of total capital per trade**.

**Stop Loss and Take Profit**
- **Stop Loss:** Automatically exits a trade to limit losses.  
- **Take Profit:** Closes a trade once a target profit is achieved.

**Risk-Reward Ratio**
- Measures expected reward compared to risk.
- A common ratio is **1:2 or higher**.

---

### c) Market Conditions and Adaptability

Markets change frequently, so strategies must adapt to different conditions.

**Regime Switching Models**
- Adjust strategies based on market conditions such as **bullish, bearish, or sideways markets**.

**Volatility Filters**
- Avoid trades during extremely high or low market volatility.

---

## Backtesting Trading Strategies

### a) Data Selection

**Historical Data**
- Includes asset price, trading volume, and sometimes fundamental indicators.

**Data Granularity**
- Intraday data  
- Daily data  
- Weekly data  
- Tick data  

The level of detail affects the accuracy of the backtest.

---

### b) Performance Metrics

**Cumulative Returns**
- Measures total return generated by the strategy during the testing period.

**Sharpe Ratio**
- Measures risk-adjusted returns by comparing returns with volatility.

**Maximum Drawdown**
- Represents the largest drop in portfolio value during the testing period.

---

### c) Avoiding Overfitting

Overfitting occurs when a strategy works well on historical data but fails in real trading.

**Out-of-Sample Testing**
- Test the model on new data that was not used during training.

**Walk-Forward Analysis**
- Continuously update parameters and test performance over different time periods.

**Monte Carlo Simulation**
- Simulates many possible market scenarios to evaluate strategy robustness.
